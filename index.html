1992

Malcolm James McCormick was born on January 19, 1992, in Point Breeze, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.

1992

He was the son of Karen Meyers, a photographer, and Mark McCormick, an architect, and had an older brother, Miller. His mother is Jewish, and his father is Christian.

1992

'07-'09

Miller originally went by the name Easy Mac and released the mixtape 'But My Mackin' Ain't Easy' in 2007 at the age of 15.

2007

By 2009, he established himself as Mac Miller, and released two mixtapes: The Jukebox: Prelude to Class Clown and The High Life.At the 2010 Pittsburgh Hip Hop Awards, Miller won 21 & Under of the Year, and Best Hip Hop Video for "Live Free".

'10-'11

2011

Miller signed with the independent label Rostrum Records in July 2010, in the lead-up to his mixtape 'K.I.D.S.', 'K.I.D.S.' was released by Rostrum in August 2010

2010

XXL featured Miller in its annual "Freshman Class" list of 2011. Miller released his fifth mixtape, Best Day Ever, in March 2011. Also in March 2011, he released a six-track EP, On and On and Beyond.

2011

Miller's debut studio album, Blue Slide Park, released on November 8, 2011. With 144,000 first week sales.

2012

2012

2012

On March 23, 2012, Miller released his seventh mixtape, Macadelic. Miller also released an EP, You, under the alias Larry Lovestein on November 21, 2012. Miller croons over lounging jazz instrumentals.

His problems got worse with his next Macadelic album and tour in 2012. The bad reviews, combined with the ongoing, grueling tour schedule, was what first drove Mac Miller to the drug scene.

“I love lean; it’s great,” Mac Miller told Complex magazine. “I was not happy and I was on lean very heavy [during the Macadelic tour.] I was so f–ed up all the time it was bad. My friends couldn’t even look at me the same. I was lost.”

2013

2013

In early 2013, Miller founded the record label imprint REMember Music, named after a deceased friend. Miller also starred in a six-episode reality series, Mac Miller and the Most Dope Family, on MTV2.

2013

On March 4, 2013, Miller released a mixtape, Run-On Sentences Vol. 1, solely featuring instrumentals made by himself. Later that month, Miller featured on singer Ariana Grande's lead single "The Way" the song is Miller's highest peak on the Billboard Hot 100 at number nine, and was certified triple platinum by the RIAA. Somewhere along the line, a 2013 Complex interview reveals, Miller became addicted to “lean,” a combination of promethazine – an addictive sedative – and codeine.

2013

Mac Miller released Watching 'Movies with the Sound Off' June 18, 2013. On September 20, 2013 Pittsburgh mayor Luke Ravenstahl presented Miller with a key to the city, and declared the date "Mac Miller Day".

2014

2014

Miller parted ways with Rostrum Records when his contract expired in January 2014. On May 11, 2014, Miller independently released his tenth solo mixtape, Faces. On Faces Miller referenced the death of another star who lost his battle with drugs: Philip Seymour Hoffman. Rapping about how "a drug habit like Philip Hoffman will probably put me in a coffin," the rapper mentioned his use of cocaine, codeine cough syrup and angel dust.

2014

In October 2014, Miller signed a recording contract and distribution deal for REMember Music with the major label Warner Bros. Records.

Pitchfork's Craig Jenkins called Faces his "most consistently honest and personal work to date". Miller later reflected on Faces, noting his drug-addled lifestyle while recording it. The second season of Miller's reality series Mac Miller and the Most Dope Family aired on MTV2 in mid-2014.

2011

2015

2015

Miller's major label debut, GO:OD AM, was released on September 18, 2015.It charted at number four on the Billboard 200, with 87,000 album-equivalent units. The album and the single "Weekend", featuring singer Miguel, were certified gold and platinum by the RIAA, respectively.

2015

In an August 2015 interview with Billboard, Miller admitted that Faces was "super depressing." "I’m not as depressed anymore. Making music when you’re depressed is great therapy, but I wanted this album to be in a different headspace," he noted, adding that he was learning to "live a little bit more."

His song "Brand Name" dropped that same month, in which he rapped, "To everyone who sell me drugs / Don't mix it with that bullshit, I'm hoping not to join the 27 Club." The 27 Club is an infamous list of musicians, artists and actors who died at age 27, including Brian Jones, Jimi Hendrix, Janis Joplin, Jim Morrison, Kurt Cobain, Amy Winehouse and Anton Yelchin.

2015

2016

2016

The Divine Feminine released on September 16, 2016. His album features Miller singing nearly as much as rapping, and incorporates genres such as R&B, jazz and funk.

2016

In a February 2016 documentary for Fader, Miller again discussed overdosing -- but admitted that he "hates being sober. I'd rather be the corny white rapper than the drugged out mess who can't even get out of his house. Overdosing is just not cool," he said. "There's no legendary romance, you don't go down in history because you overdosed. You just die."

2018

2018

Miller's fifth studio album, Swimming, was released on August 3, 2018, to positive reviews from critics. Pitchfork described the album as consisting of "wistful soul and warm funk", through his exploration of heartbreak and his own mental health issues. Swimming debuted at number three on the Billboard 200 and was nominated for Best Rap Album at the 61st Annual Grammy Awards.

2018

Miller rapped about those hurt feelings in his last performance, on Sept. 3 at the Hotel Cafe in Los Angeles. Rest In Power Malcom James McCmormick.

On Aug. 13 depression was at the top of Miller's mind in his interview with Vulture, “I really wouldn’t want just happiness,And I don’t want just sadness either. I don’t want to be depressed. I want to be able to have good days and bad days... I can’t imagine not waking up sometimes and being like, ‘I don’t feel like doing shit.’ And then having days where you wake up and you feel on top of the world.”

This timeline is composed of Foundation elements, coupled with CSS and Javascript aspects to culminate the history and timeline of the great and late, Mac Miller. Miller, was more than just a rapper, he was a musician, an artist, a producer, and a genuine kind soul. Many kids that were growing up in 2007-2010 were growing up with Mac Miller. He, first came on the scene through his exceptional youtube videos and obvious lyrical talent. At first some people seen him as 'another white rapper'. However, that didn't seem to bother him that much. His early music consisted of smoking weed, partying, girls, and having fun. With this type of music, there were many people that were growing up doing the same things as him. After he became an established rapper, he became introudced to the drug scene, this was a hard point in time for Miller. He was evolving and adapting his style to a new vibration. Miller was breaking out of the 'frat white boy rap' and showed he was more than that. He started perfecting his craft, locking himself in his studio for days creating and creating and creating.

Miller, started explaining his life through his music, he wasn't afraid to speak on how he was doing. He explicitly spoke about his drug abuse, not only to release his bottled emotions through this outlet, but also to exclaim to the world 'This is me! I am human!' With this type of personal connection between an artist and his fans, people could relate to his music. Mac Miller was not perfect, however no-one is. He showed the world that it is O.K. to hurt, it is 0.K. to struggle. Moving forward Miller was focused on becoming a better person, recovering, and also perfecting his music. As he released more music, he became a more updated version of himself everytime. His music became less depressing and more uplifting, of course with the cynical and sublime lyrics slipped into pieces. He became wealthy and successful, he gained millions off his music, and saved millions of people going through the same struggle. Sadly, in the end millions couldn't save him.
